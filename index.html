Hi! I'm Xueyu Zhang

I'm a PhD researcher in the Inamura Lab of Tokyo Institute of Technology. Our research group focuses on the fascinating field of kink microstructures in layered materials. My job in this group is to use differential geometry, continuum mechanics and finite element analysis to modelize the formation and the geometry of these microstructures. By validating and refining the geometry model using techniques like Electron Back-Scatter Diffraction (EBSD) and Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) observations, I aim to establish a useful geometric model for the researches regarding "kink strengthening" mecanism - a new design guideline for the light-weight, high-strength structural materials. Ultimately, I hope to contribute to the advancement of materials science and help create a more sustainable future. In my free time, I enjoy break dancing and relaxing to jazz-hiphop or 1990s city pop music.
